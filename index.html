Stock Up!

A Grocery Shopping and Meal Planning App

Stock Up is a concept, design and prototype for an app that helps large families and institutions such as group homes grocery shop in a much more efficient way. It was made as part of my post-graduate studies in UX design at York University. The main premise of Stock Up is that it takes in your personal recipes, and recipes from others and builds you an ultimate grocery list. Additional secondary features included a recipe view to follow instructions in-kitchen, and the ability to track your carbon ‘foodprint’. The design began in low-fidelity on humble paper. I user-tested, collaborated and refined it and it made its way into a beautiful, coloured and interactive Figma prototype. Go ahead and scroll/click! The project taught me a lot about virtual user testing.

  • My Contribution: Concept, Design, Interaction, User Testing,
  • Tools Used: Figma, Photoshop, Useberry
  • Date: March 2020

Happy Camper

Stratgey Card Game For Two

Having always loved board and card games pushed me to create Happy Camper with the help of Chris Adkins. The game takes inspiration from Netrunner, with original mechanics and art direction. It is unique in that it is asymmetrical- one person plays as nature, and one as the camper. Though the art is the most visible contribution, there was a lot of play-testing and spreadsheets to get the card ratios correct.

  • My Contribution: Design, Concepts, User Testing
  • Tools Used: Photoshop, Excel
  • Date: Nov. 2018

Motion Specialties

Learning Asset Design and and Fabricating Custom Solutions For Wheelchair Clients

I worked at a company called Motion Specialties for 3 years from 2017-2020! Unfortunately I had to leave because of chronic back pain, but I treasured my time there. I worked on a team to modify accessibility equipment, often for the severely disabled. There I gained experience in CAD(computer aided design) with Fusion360, machining, industrial sewing and 3d scanning. I also designed training and how-to guides and assets for the team. Posted above is one such tutorial thrown together describing how I would make footplate walls for some power chairs to prevent peoples’ feet from slipping off of the footplate into the castors/wheels.

Motion really taught me how to think on my toes- we even had to think of and build solutions while clients were waiting sometimes! As I transition into a less physical design role, I will always treasure my time at Motion and the optimism of even the most severely disabled people.

AccessTO

Accessibility Audits: Interviewing, Writing, Photography

2018-2019 I volunteered as an accessibility auditor for them. The position involved visiting restaurants and cafes in the GTA, taking pictures and measurements of their doorways, hallways, bathrooms, etc. and informally interviewing staff. Afterwards I would write the copy for the blog post as well. See an example of a post I authored HERE

Ontario has general accessibility laws and codes for newer buildings, but people have varying degrees of disability. For example, one threshold step might be OK for some people, but not others. AccessTO provides the disabled population with detailed venue information so they can make informed decisions about traveling there independently.

Visual Art

Charcoal

Traditional visual art has always been close to my heart, and a way of reducing screen-time and resting my eyes. I think there are huge opportunities to make designs this way and scan them in and manipulate them digitally as well. Simple pen and paper is a great way to make wireframes and user flows because there is less between you and the idea- to make a shape you just draw it, you don't have to worry about finding a shape tool, or aligning objects- it's freeing.
